About me

For the last 20 years I have worked as a teacher, editor and project manager. I have taught academic English in China, Japan, Afghanistan and Brazil, as well as running the English for Academic Purposes course at LSHTM. Working for the ELT School in London, I have taught businessmen and women from Hitachi Europe, Daiwa, Mitsubishi and numerous others. I am lucky to be able to establish a good rapport with students easily and my years of experience enable me to develop teaching materials that are entirely appropriate for my students, whether they are receiving individual tuition or group training. Many of my students have commented on their surprise at how interesting learning English can be.

My editing skills began with training at Book House in 1987 and were honed on numerous PhD theses (including my own) and Masters dissertations. These days, in addition to theses and dissertations I edit journal articles, slide presentations and technical reports by students and academics from all over the world. I also edit technical reports for WHO, ICDDR,B and the Malaria Consortium.
